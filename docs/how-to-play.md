# How to Play Vocabulary Quest

Vocabulary Quest helps you learn and remember SAT-level vocabulary words. It works in two ways: learning new words from books you're reading, and practicing words you've already learned.

---

## The home screen

When you open the app, you'll see:

- **Practice banner** (green) — if you have words ready to practice, this shows the count. Click it to start a practice session.
- **"All caught up!"** — if no words are due, this tells you when your next practice will be.
- **Your books** — books you've uploaded. Click a book to learn new words from it.
- **Upload a book** — drag and drop an EPUB or TXT file to add a new book.

---

## Learning new words from a book

This is how you add words to your vocabulary:

1. **Upload a book** — EPUB files work best (they have accurate chapter breaks). TXT files work too.

2. **Story Bible** — the first time you open a book, the app reads it and identifies characters and settings. This makes the illustrations consistent throughout.

3. **Pick a chapter** — choose which chapter to study.

4. **Choose your words** — the app suggests ~20 vocabulary words from the chapter. You can toggle words on/off. The app pre-selects 5 to start with.

5. **Play the game** — three rounds for each word:
   - **Meaning** — "What does this word mean?" Multiple choice with 4 options. If you guess wrong, you get a hint and can try again.
   - **Fill in the blank** — the definition is shown, and you pick which word fits a blank in the original passage.
   - **Spelling** — trace the word letter by letter, then spell it from memory.

6. **Results** — see how you did on each word across all three rounds. First-try correct answers get ✦, answers with hints get ◆.

After you finish, the words enter your review queue. The app schedules them for future practice based on how well you did.

---

## Practicing words you've learned

Practice sessions pull from all your words — from every book you've read and any words that have been seeded into your vocabulary.

**How practice works:**

- Click the "Practice N words" banner on the home screen
- You'll get 5 words per session
- Each word gets **one exercise**, chosen based on how well you know it:
  - **New/weak words** → multiple choice meaning (easiest)
  - **Familiar words** → fill in the blank (moderate)
  - **Strong words** → spelling (hardest)
- Exercise types are mixed together, not grouped
- After the session, you can start another one for more words

**How the scheduling works:**

The app uses spaced repetition (the same system as Anki). Words you get right are scheduled further into the future. Words you struggle with come back sooner. The goal is to practice each word right before you'd forget it.

- Get it right first try → comes back in a longer interval (1 day → 6 days → 15 days → ...)
- Get it right with hints → still counts, but the interval grows more slowly
- Get it wrong → resets to tomorrow

Over time, well-known words fade into the background (reviewed once a month or less) while difficult words stay in frequent rotation.

---

## What the scores mean

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ✦ | Correct on first try — perfect recall |
| ◆ | Correct after a hint — you knew it but needed a nudge |

Both count as "correct" for spaced repetition — the word advances to a longer interval either way. But first-try answers build the interval faster.

---

## Tips

- **Short, frequent sessions beat long, rare ones.** Five words a day is better than 30 words once a week.
- **Don't skip practice days.** The spaced repetition algorithm is designed around regular review. Missing a day means overdue words pile up.
- **It's fine to get things wrong.** The system adapts — wrong answers just mean you'll see that word again sooner.
- **Read the passage context.** The book passages aren't just decoration — seeing the word used in context builds deeper understanding than memorizing a definition.
